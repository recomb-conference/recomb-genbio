We are excited to announce the RECOMB Satellite Conference on Generative Models for Biology and Health. As the recent Nobel Prize in Chemistry demonstrated, large pretrained models are revolutionizing biology. While protein structure is likely the most mature area right now, several such models are being developed spanning all levels of biology from genes and molecules to cells to tissues and all the way to human phenotypes. These models cover text, images, video, speech and have also been applied to gene expression and other dynamic properties.

While some of this work is relevant to existing conferences, including RECOMB and ISMB/ECCB, there are also unique aspects to this area that necessitates a new conference solely focused on large-scale AI for biology. First, detailed discussion of technical details and architectures that underlie such large-scale models is often outside the scope of existing computational biology conferences but is critical to the success of models for biomedical data. Technical discussions, including various computational platforms, databases, representation and processing are also not always of interest to the more general computational biology community but are definitely on the minds of those working in this area. And, of course, any application or use case of such models in biology and medicine is of interest. Finally, and importantly, integration of different models to obtain a better understanding of complete biological systems are topics that can only be discussed in a dedicated conference.

While some leading ML conferences now include workshops focused on generative AI for Biology, these are often very technical. The unique integration of biology and computer science which has long been the tradition of the RECOMB conference series is the most appropriate venue to discuss the ground breaking work in this area.

We look forward to seeing you in Boston!
